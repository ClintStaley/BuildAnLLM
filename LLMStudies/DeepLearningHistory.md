# Deep Learning History Study

Review the LLMStudy.docx file provided at the top level of the course. Following its guidelines, and using Claude, build an LLM study on the history of "deep learning", aka "neural networks" and in the 1960s "perceptrons"

## List of topics:
1. Origins of the neural network or perceptron concept. 
2. History of the backpropagation algorithm.
3. The two "AI winters" in which development of neural networks stalled.  Why they stalled, and what caused their resurgence in the 1980s and in the past 15 years.
4. Innovators/contributors of importance from 1960s to present e.g. Hopfield, Hinton, Sutskever, LeCun, Amodei.
5. The elements driving to the most recent resurgence, including GPUs, large volumes of training data, improved training algorithms, and a strong research community.
6. Examples of "s-curve" tech growth in other areas such as aerospace in the 20th century, and discussion of whether the same may apply to the current AI trend, and how soon if so.

And a reminder for this and all future LLM Studies: you need deep and interactive exploration of even these simple topics, per the LLM Study Guide.  Quizzes based on the study will anticipate this.
